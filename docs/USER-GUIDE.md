# User Guide – Streamer Scheduler

[Español](GUIA-USUARIOS.md)

Information on how to use **Streamer Scheduler**: what the app is, how to get started, and how to use its main features.

---

## What is Streamer Scheduler?

Streamer Scheduler is a web application that lets you **schedule and manage posts** for multiple social networks from one place. It is designed for content creators and streamers who want to plan content for Twitch, X (Twitter), Instagram, and Discord without logging into each platform separately.

---

## Getting started

### Create an account and sign in

- You can **sign up with email and password** or **sign in with Google or Twitch**.
- After your first login, if you don’t have a license, you may be assigned a **free trial** (depending on the application’s configuration).
- Once signed in, you’ll see the **Dashboard** (main panel).

### Licenses

The application uses a license system:

- **Trial** – Time-limited use.
- **Monthly / Quarterly / Lifetime** – Depending on what the service offers.
- Your **license** type and expiry date are shown in your **Profile** and, if available, in the billing section.
- You can **purchase or renew** licenses from the app (Stripe payments) when this is enabled.

---

## Dashboard

On the **Dashboard** (/dashboard) you have:

- A summary of your activity and basic stats (e.g. scheduled or published posts).
- Quick links to **schedule content** and your **profile**.
- If Twitch is connected, subscription, bits, or donation data may be shown (when enabled by the administrator).
- Important notices (e.g. license or password reminders).

From here you can open the **calendar** and create posts.

---

## Scheduling content (Calendar)

The **Schedule** or **Calendar** section (/schedule) is where you create and organize content:

- **Calendar view** by day/week: you see scheduled posts and can **drag and drop** to change date or time.
- **Create a post**: title, text, and **platform** selection (Twitch, X, Instagram, Discord, etc., as enabled).
- **Upload images or videos** to attach to the post (within your plan’s limits).
- **Date and time** for publishing: the system will attempt to publish at that time on the selected platforms (when they are connected and the feature is active).
- You can **edit** or **delete** posts from the calendar or from the post’s detail view.

For posts to be sent automatically to each network, you must have those platforms’ accounts **connected** (see below).

---

## Profile

On your **Profile** (/profile) you can:

- View and **edit your name**, bio, timezone, and language.
- Change your **profile photo**.
- View your **license** (type and expiry date).
- Manage **connected accounts** (Google, Twitch, X/Twitter, Discord): connect or disconnect to publish on each network.
- View **post performance analytics**: total published, failed, and attempts per platform (Post Performance Analytics).
- Change your **password** (Security tab).
- Adjust **notifications**, **theme** (light/dark), and **language** (Spanish/English) when the app offers these options.

---

## Connecting platforms

For content to be published on each network, you need to **link** your accounts:

- Under **Profile → Connected accounts** (or equivalent) you’ll see which networks are connected or not.
- Click **Connect** on the one you want (e.g. Twitch, X, Discord). The official authorization page for that platform will open; sign in and accept the permissions.
- Once connected, you can select that platform when creating or editing a post in the calendar.
- If an account stops working (expired token, etc.), the app may ask you to **disconnect and reconnect** that account.

## Templates (when available)

If the application has **templates**:

- They let you reuse content structures (text, hashtags, etc.).
- You can create templates in the relevant section and apply them when creating a new post in the calendar to save time.
